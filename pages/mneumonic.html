Mneumonics - a password generator

Create passwords that are hard to crack but easy to memorize using an age-old trick.

How does someone who is not a savant remember a random-ish password such as the following?

AtKhaatKmcp@Ht4a
For generations, the phrase "Every good boy does fine" has helped fledgling musicians remember the music scale - EGBDF. In this case, the above password is derived from the first letters of the following phrase with a number and special character added to boost randomness. 
All the King's horses and all the King's men couldn't put Humpty together again!

Enter a phrase, sentence or several sentences into the box below and the app will take the first letters from each word and then also ensure that at least 1 number and 1 special character is included in the passphrase. Click the Submit button to generate a passphrase, click again to generate another variation. The details and rationale behind the process are covered in the TL;DR article Mneumonic password guide.

The generated password should be regarded as a suggestion, and should be modified by the person before using as an actual password. Even though this app does not send any data over the Internet during the generation and checking of the password, any Internet app should be regarded as insecure when it comes to the protection of your vital access credentials.
